\section{Legal, Social, Ethical and Professional Issues \& Considerations}
\label{sec:ethics}

\subsection{Ethical Consent and Human Participation}
The project includes two human-participation activities: the preliminary survey (completed) and UAT on Evaluation Day (planned). Both involve low-risk tasks: answering questions about planning habits and a trial run of the software, respectively. Moreover, participants are students from Warwick DCS who are aware of this third-year project and the author's personal network (friends and family). Also, the responses are collected anonymously, and results are reported in aggregate statistics. Therefore, ethical consent is not required. However, in the future, if this project becomes commercial, a more robust study would be required. In this case, the survey and UAT must consider a larger sample space and, where applicable, formal consent procedures.

\subsection{Data Protection and Privacy}
\textit{GiTrip} minimises personal data collection and avoids sensitive categories. For user accounts, only the essential identifiers (email/username) are stored, along with hashed passwords. Access control is enforced via repository visibility and collaborator roles. Users can request the export or deletion of their data, in accordance with GDPR principles~\cite{gdpr}.

\subsection{Third-party Services and Legal Compliance}
\textit{GiTrip} follows the usage policy of OpenStreetMap, Openrouteservice, Nominatim, Google Directions API, GNews API, and Open-Meteo.
